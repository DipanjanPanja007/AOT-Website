Overview

Welcome to our college's "Program Structure" page, your gateway to exploring the diverse range of academic offerings we provide. Whether you're interested in pursuing undergraduate or postgraduate studies, this page serves as a comprehensive guide to understanding the structure, curriculum, and admission process for our programs.

Discover our array of undergraduate programs, including Computer Science and Engineering, Electrical and Electronics Engineering, Electronics and Communication Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Computer Science and Business Systems. Each program is meticulously crafted to provide students with a solid foundation in their chosen field, preparing them for successful careers in the ever-evolving technological landscape.

Admission guide

The score obtained in Class 12th

  • Students need to score good marks in the 12th final year examination.
  • A minimum of 45% of marks are required to get in 12th to become eligible for appearing in the entrance examination.
  • SC/ST students need to get a minimum of 40% of marks in the 12th examination.

Clear the cut-offs for the entrance examinations

  • AOT conduct the admission process based on the scores obtained in the entrance examination.
  • So, apart from scoring good marks in the 12th examination, clearing cut-offs of the entrance examination is most essential for admission.

Final Selection for B. Tech Course

  • Final selection of the students based on the marks obtained in the 12th examination, scores obtained in the entrance examination, and personal interview round.

How to Apply?

The process of admission is as follows: -

  • First, students need to register their names.
  • After completing the registration process, students will receive an OTP in their registered mobile number.
  • Students need to log in again and then fill the complete application form.
  • The admission fees need to be paid online.
  • After the admission process completes successfully, students will get a confirmation receipt.
  • Take a printout of the receipt for future use.

UG Admission

    WBJEE

  • West Bengal conducts the JEE entrance exam every year.
  • Students must complete the 12th examination from any recognized board with a minimum of 45% of aggregate marks.
  • The nationality of the students must be Indian.
  • Entrance exams are conducted online.

    JEE Main

  • JEE is conducted four times a year. In February, March, April, and May JEE Main is conducted every year.
  • Students must complete the 12th examination from any recognized board a minimum of 45% of aggregate marks in graduation.
  • The nationality of the students must be Indian.
  • Entrance exams are conducted online.

Research Facilities

Advanced Materials Laboratory

Description: Equipped with state-of-the-art instruments and specialized equipment, the Advanced Materials Laboratory facilitates research in the development and characterization of advanced materials for various applications, including nanotechnology, energy storage, and biomedical engineering. Researchers have access to scanning electron microscopes, atomic force microscopes, X-ray diffraction systems, and high-performance computing resources.

Location: Engineering Building, Room 301

Contact: Dr. Emily Johnson, Director of Advanced Materials Laboratory

Biomedical Imaging Center

Description: The Biomedical Imaging Center houses advanced imaging technologies for non-invasive visualization and analysis of biological systems at the cellular and molecular level. Researchers utilize cutting-edge imaging modalities such as confocal microscopy, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), positron emission tomography (PET), and single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) to investigate physiological processes, disease mechanisms, and therapeutic interventions.

Location: Life Sciences Building, Room 201

Contact: Dr. Michael Brown, Director of Biomedical Imaging Center

Environmental Monitoring Station

Description: The Environmental Monitoring Station is equipped with a comprehensive array of sensors and monitoring instruments for real-time environmental data collection and analysis. Researchers use this facility to study air quality, water pollution, soil contamination, and ecological dynamics in diverse ecosystems. The station is equipped with meteorological stations, water quality analyzers, gas chromatographs, and remote sensing technologies.

Location: Environmental Science Building, Room 101

Contact: Dr. Sophia Lee, Director of Environmental Monitoring Station
