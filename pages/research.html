Research Facilities

Advanced Materials Laboratory

Description: Equipped with state-of-the-art instruments and specialized equipment, the Advanced Materials Laboratory facilitates research in the development and characterization of advanced materials for various applications, including nanotechnology, energy storage, and biomedical engineering. Researchers have access to scanning electron microscopes, atomic force microscopes, X-ray diffraction systems, and high-performance computing resources.

Location: Engineering Building, Room 301

Contact: Dr. Emily Johnson, Director of Advanced Materials Laboratory

Biomedical Imaging Center

Description: The Biomedical Imaging Center houses advanced imaging technologies for non-invasive visualization and analysis of biological systems at the cellular and molecular level. Researchers utilize cutting-edge imaging modalities such as confocal microscopy, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), positron emission tomography (PET), and single-photon emission computed tomography (SPECT) to investigate physiological processes, disease mechanisms, and therapeutic interventions.

Location: Life Sciences Building, Room 201

Contact: Dr. Michael Brown, Director of Biomedical Imaging Center

Environmental Monitoring Station

Description: The Environmental Monitoring Station is equipped with a comprehensive array of sensors and monitoring instruments for real-time environmental data collection and analysis. Researchers use this facility to study air quality, water pollution, soil contamination, and ecological dynamics in diverse ecosystems. The station is equipped with meteorological stations, water quality analyzers, gas chromatographs, and remote sensing technologies.

Location: Environmental Science Building, Room 101

Contact: Dr. Sophia Lee, Director of Environmental Monitoring Station
